The chemicals used to make plastics can be absorbed by the human body. Ninety-three percent of Americans aged 6 or older tested positive for BPA, a widely used plastic chemical, according to one survey by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. (Keep Scrolling)

Plastic bags are usually made of polyethylene, a non-biodegradable plastic created from non-renewable crude oil and natural gas. The fossil fuels needed to produce just 14 plastic bags could drive a car for a mile.

Under 10 percent of plastic bags get recycled, according to some estimates. The rest end up in landfills or as litter on the streets, or in the oceans.

Americans use 1,500 plastic bags a year!
