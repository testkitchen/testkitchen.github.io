The chemicals used to make plastics can be absorbed by the human body.Ninety-three percent of Americans aged 6 or older tested positive for BPA, a widely used plastic chemical, according to one survey by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention.

Plastics in the oceans also pose a threat to over 600 different wildlife species. One in three leatherback sea turtles have been found with plastic in their stomachs. In 2017, a dead whale beached on Norway’s coast had 30 plastic bags inside its belly.
